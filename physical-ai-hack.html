What is this

AI that touches
the real world.

No toy demos. No chatbots. Real hardware, real sensors, real impact.

The Physical AI Hackathon is a curated, in-person build experience designed to bring together developers, makers, and engineers to create AI-powered systems that interact with the physical world.

The event focuses on bridging the gap between software intelligence and real-world applications, encouraging participants to build tangible, working prototypes using AI and hardware.

Physical AI means systems that capture real-world inputs through sensors, process data using AI/ML models (Edge AI, TinyML, Computer Vision), and trigger meaningful actions through physical outputs or devices.

Teams of 2–4 are curated through an application process based on prior experience in hardware, software, or AI/ML. The event begins with a focused session on Edge AI fundamentals.

FAQs

Questions?

What is Physical AI?+
Physical AI refers to systems that capture real-world inputs through sensors, process data using AI/ML models (Edge AI, TinyML, Computer Vision), and trigger meaningful actions through physical outputs or devices. All projects must demonstrate a clear interaction between AI and the physical environment.
Who can participate?+
30–40 curated participants in teams of 2–4, selected through an application process based on prior experience in hardware, software, or AI/ML. The selection process maintains a balance between technical depth and collaborative learning. Whether you're strong in hardware or software, apply — teams are cross-disciplinary.
What hardware and resources will be provided?+
Shared hardware resources available on-site including XIAO-ESP32S3 Sense, XIAO nRF52840 Sense, and Grove Vision AI modules. Hardware inventory will be published in advance for planning. Participants are encouraged to bring additional components. Continuous mentorship support throughout the event. Hardware partner: MakerGram.
How do I register?+
Registration opens April 4th and closes April 12th. Apply individually or as a team. Selected participants will be notified by April 13th. Teams of 2–4 will work together during the 24-hour event starting April 18th at 2 PM.
What are the prerequisites?+
Prior experience in at least one of: hardware/embedded systems, software development, or AI/ML. Teams are ideally cross-disciplinary. The hackathon begins with a focused session introducing Edge AI fundamentals and approaches to building AI models for constrained hardware environments.

Ready to build?

30–40 curated spots only. Applications reviewed based on experience in hardware, software, or AI/ML. Apply before April 12th.
